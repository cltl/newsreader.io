On  January 30, the NewsReader project will organise a hackathon in London. On January 21st the first January 2015 hackathon took place in the Amsterdam public library.
On this page, you can find all you need to know about these events.
Our hackathons may be interesting to you if:

- You’re a data journalist on an automotive desk;
- You’re an analyst sifting daily news looking for information on your company or on competitors;
- You’re a data analyst looking to understand how your customers operate their supply chain
- You’re an analyst trying to find secondary events that could influence an investment decision;

During the event, you get to play with data that has been processed using the NewsReader processing pipeline and you can ask the NewsReader team members about the technology.
To find out more about the different facets of the NewsReader project, we have prepared the following blog posts:

- [The NewsReader data](http://wordpress.let.vupr.nl/newsreader/more-than-a-waste-paper-bin/ "More than a waste paper bin: the data for From Porsches to Pizza")
- [The developers' story](http://wordpress.let.vupr.nl/newsreader/newsreader-the-developers-story/ "NewsReader: the developers story")
- [The NewsReader technology stack](http://wordpress.let.vupr.nl/newsreader/the-technology-behind-from-porsches-to-pizza/ "The Technology Behind “From Porsches to Pizza”")
- [Visualising the NewsReader data](http://wordpress.let.vupr.nl/newsreader/diving-into-the-nwr-data/ "Diving into the NewsReader data")
